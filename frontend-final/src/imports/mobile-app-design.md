Design a modern mobile healthcare app called **“Can We Get a Lung?”** that performs a quick respiratory screening using short voice recordings and simple health inputs. The app should feel trustworthy like a medical tool but also engaging and slightly playful so users don’t feel intimidated. Use soft healthcare colors (blue, green, white) with rounded cards, friendly icons, and subtle breathing/lung illustrations or emoji-style characters.

The design should feel like a polished Apple Health–style app with smooth layout, clean typography, soft shadows, and intuitive navigation.

Create a **complete mobile app flow** with the following screens:

---

### 1. Welcome / Home Screen

* App title: **“Can We Get a Lung?”**
* Subtitle: “A quick respiratory check using your voice.”
* Friendly lung mascot or breathing-themed illustration
* Primary button: **Start Check**
* Bottom navigation bar with:

  * Home
  * History
  * Profile

---

### 2. Instructions Screen

Explain that the test takes **about 30 seconds** and includes three short voice tasks.

Display the tasks as **three clean cards with icons**:

1. **Cough Test**

   * “Please cough once clearly when prompted.”

2. **Sustained Vowel**

   * “Say ‘Ahhh’ steadily for about 10 seconds.”

3. **Breathing Test**

   * “Take a deep breath and slowly exhale for about 10 seconds.”

Include a progress indicator showing **3 steps total**.

Button: **Begin Test**

---

### 3. Recording Screen – Step 1: Cough

* Large microphone button
* Instruction text: “Please cough clearly once.”
* Visual waveform responding to audio
* Circular **10-second progress timer**
* Friendly animated lung mascot encouraging the user

---

### 4. Recording Screen – Step 2: Sustained Vowel

* Instruction: “Take a breath and say ‘Ahhh’ for 10 seconds.”
* Same UI style as previous step
* Waveform visualization
* Progress indicator: **Step 2 of 3**

---

### 5. Recording Screen – Step 3: Breathing

* Instruction: “Take a deep breath and slowly exhale.”
* Calm breathing animation (expanding/contracting circle)
* 10-second timer
* Progress indicator: **Step 3 of 3**

---

### 6. Health Information Screen

Collect simple demographic and health inputs.

Fields should include:

* Age
* Sex at birth
* Nicotine consumption
* Existing health conditions
* Allergies
* City where the user lives

Use **dropdowns, toggle buttons, and card inputs** to make the form quick and mobile-friendly.

Button: **Analyze Results**

---

### 7. Processing Screen

Show a loading screen with:

* Animated lungs breathing
* Text: “Analyzing your respiratory signals…”
* Friendly AI-style assistant or breathing character

Make this screen visually engaging so it feels like real analysis is happening.

---

### 8. Results Screen – Low Risk

Use a **green background theme**.

Display a reassuring message:

“Everything looks fine.
No immediate signs suggesting you need to go to the ER.”

Include:

* happy lung mascot
* checkmark icon
* buttons:

  * Run Another Check
  * View History

---

### 9. Results Screen – Elevated Risk

Use a **yellow caution theme**.

Display message:

“Something doesn’t feel quite right.
You may want to consult with a doctor.”

Include:

* caution icon
* supportive tone (not alarming)
* buttons:

  * Learn Next Steps
  * Return Home

---

### 10. History Screen

Show previous respiratory checks as cards including:

* Date
* Risk result
* Quick summary

Color-code results subtly (green / yellow).

---

### Design Style

* Modern healthcare UI
* Rounded corners and soft shadows
* Friendly but professional tone
* Clean typography
* Smooth spacing and card-based layout
* Slight playful elements like lung mascots or breathing animations
* Colors:

  * Primary blue
  * Health green
  * Yellow caution state
  * White/light backgrounds

The design should feel like a **real, polished mobile health app suitable for a hackathon demo or startup prototype.**
